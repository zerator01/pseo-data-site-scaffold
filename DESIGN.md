# Tarot Card Art Direction

## Core Philosophy

This deck is not a collage of unrelated fantasy illustrations. It is a tightly controlled visual system.

Every card must feel like it belongs to the same premium black-and-gold tarot deck:

- Strict duotone only: deep black background plus fine gold line art
- No accent colors: no blue, purple, green, red, white highlights, or multicolor lighting
- Vertical tarot card proportion with rounded corners
- A consistent Art Nouveau ornamental frame with organic vines, floral curls, and flowing linework
- A single thick ornate frame only; no second outer border, no extra glowing perimeter, no double-framing, no black channel between border layers
- A dedicated empty title cartouche at the bottom center inside the card frame
- Subtle gold glow on key focal elements only, never explosive HDR bloom

## Base Prompt Formula

The most stable deck consistency comes from a short, rigid prompt skeleton rather than a long corrective prompt.

Use this base formula:

`A tarot card illustration for '[CARD NAME]'. Masterpiece following the 'Gilded Shadow' design philosophy. Abyssal obsidian background, anchored by an opulent, meticulously crafted geometric gold border. [SYMBOLIC SCENE DESCRIPTION]. No text, no words, no letters. Museum-quality, masterfully crafted.`

This formula should remain structurally stable across cards. Change only:

- card name
- central symbolic scene description

Do not constantly re-invent the style prompt per card. Consistency comes from repeating the same architectural sentence structure.

## Composition Rules

- Always render the full card as a finished object, not a scene that bleeds off the edges
- Keep the subject centered and readable at thumbnail size
- The border must be stylistically consistent across cards
- The border must remain elegant and controlled; it should feel richly mystical without competing with the subject
- Use only one border system: a single integrated inner frame that contains all ornamentation
- The side margins should be occupied by the border itself: dense, intricate gold ornamentation with no empty black gutter between outer edge and inner frame
- The bottom title cartouche must remain empty for later typography overlay and should be organic, not a rigid rectangle
- Background inside the frame should stay mostly black with sparse gold detail, so the central figure remains dominant

## Style Rules

- Fine metallic line engraving, not painterly brushwork
- Museum-grade occult luxury, not game-card fantasy art
- Geometric gold border with controlled ornamental richness, matching the most successful prior cards
- Ornament should be dense but disciplined: celestial line motifs, occult curls, rays, crescents, stars, and flowing filigree are welcome
- Avoid introducing specific plants, fruit clusters, or decorative motifs so literal that they hijack the card identity
- Glow should feel like gold leaf catching candlelight
- Symmetry and balance matter, but the central figure can retain graceful motion

## Negative Rules

Do not generate:

- Colored fire, leaves, gems, skies, or clothing
- White paper texture or parchment backgrounds
- Text, letters, roman numerals, captions, or signatures
- Missing or inconsistent title panel
- A rigid rectangular title box
- Mixed border languages from different styles
- A second outer border or luminous perimeter outside the main frame
- A black gap or channel between two border layers
